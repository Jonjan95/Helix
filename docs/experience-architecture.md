# Helix experience architecture

This document defines the narrative structure of Helix: what the visitor encounters, understands, and feels from arrival to exit. It is the primary reference for experience sequencing, storytelling, emotional pacing, and information flow.

The blueprint is intentionally independent of implementation. Future visual and technical decisions should serve this journey rather than determine it.

## Introduction

Helix is not a traditional portfolio or a collection of separate pages. It is a guided, continuous experience in which each chapter prepares the visitor for the next.

## Experience goals

- Slow the visitor down.
- Reward curiosity.
- Build trust gradually.
- Demonstrate quality through execution.
- Let the visitor discover rather than consume.
- Avoid overwhelming the visitor.

## Experience timeline

### Chapter 1: Arrival

The visitor meets the laptop as the entrance to Helix.

### Chapter 2: Orientation

The visitor begins to understand Jonathan's identity and professional direction.

### Chapter 3: Engineering mindset

The journey explains how Jonathan approaches quality, testing, reliability, problem solving, curiosity, and craftsmanship.

### Chapter 4: Selected work

Projects turn the stated mindset into concrete stories and decisions.

### Chapter 5: Proof

Evidence builds trust through engineering artefacts, testing, architecture, documentation, and public work.

### Chapter 6: Future

The journey concludes with curiosity, direction, and an invitation to continue the conversation.

## Emotional curve

The experience develops from curiosity into confidence, focus, renewed energy, and an open-ended sense of possibility.

## Information density

Information begins sparse and gradually becomes more detailed as the visitor gains context and trust.

## Motion preparation

Future motion should reinforce progression, spatial continuity, and attention without becoming necessary for understanding.

## Design relationship

The design system defines how Helix looks and behaves. The experience architecture defines how the same system should feel over time.

## Future implementation order

Future work should establish narrative clarity and static transitions before attempting camera movement, spatial reveals, or micro interactions.
